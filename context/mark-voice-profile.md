# Mark's Voice Profile

How Mark actually talks, pulled from three real clips of him speaking unscripted (Cool Hollow Solutions content, June 2026). This is separate from `voice-and-tone.md`, which is the general Cool Hollow Coaching brand voice for written copy. Use this file specifically when writing anything Mark will say on camera: hook scripts, video lessons, the VSL. When something changes or a new clip surfaces a pattern that contradicts this, update it rather than guessing.

---

## The short version

Mark talks like someone who's run a real business for years and is mid-conversation with a business owner across a table, not presenting from a script. Comma-heavy, list-driven, repeats key words for emphasis, checks in with "right?" constantly, and trails one thought into the next rather than landing on a hard stop.

---

## Specific patterns observed

**The "right?" check-in.** He punctuates a claim with "right?" as a verbal handshake, not a real question. It shows up after almost every key point, it's the closest thing to a verbal tic in his delivery. Worth including in scripts at a similar rate, not stripping it out for polish.

**Rhythmic lists for effect.** When making a point about being overloaded, he stacks roles or ideas in a fast list rather than one descriptor: naming off CEO, CFO, and several other hats in a row. This list-stacking is a real device, use it when a script needs to land "you're doing everything."

**Repeats the key word, doesn't just say it once.** When he lands on the word that matters (alignment, collaboration, intentional, genius), he says it more than once in close proximity, almost like circling back to underline it, rather than varying the vocabulary for elegance.

**Opens an idea with "I think..." or "Typically, when you're a [role]...".** He generalizes to the audience's situation before getting specific, rather than opening cold on the specific claim. This softens the directness slightly and reads more like shared experience than a lecture.

**Short rhetorical questions, back to back.** "What's your genius? What's your brilliance?" Two short questions in a row land harder than one longer one.

**Trails forward rather than landing a hard close.** A thought often ends on a forward-looking payoff ("what your company is going to grow is going to be exponentially more...") rather than a clean period, like he's already moving to the next idea before fully closing the last one.

**Genuinely conversational grammar, not cleaned up.** Casual contractions and informal grammar show up naturally and don't read as a mistake, they read as a real person talking, not a script being read. Don't over-correct this when writing for him.

---

## The tension worth knowing about

Mark's natural vocabulary includes words the brand voice guide explicitly avoids in writing, "synergies," "collaboration," "alignment" showed up in real, unscripted speech. That's not a contradiction to fix, it's a real difference between how he talks and how the brand writes. On camera, his actual voice carries the credibility, a scripted line that sounds like it scrubbed out every word he'd really say will read as fake. The call to make script by script: keep his real cadence and his real go-to words, but don't hand him a script that leans on jargon as a crutch instead of a specific, concrete point. When in doubt, write the line the way the three clips above actually sound, not the way the general brand guide would write it on a page.
