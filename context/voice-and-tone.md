# Voice and Tone: Cool Hollow Coaching

This is how Cool Hollow Coaching sounds. Everything I write, scripts, emails, web copy, captions, follows this. It is Matt's voice for a US business-owner audience. When you correct something, I learn from it and update this file.

---

## The short version

Direct, warm, encouraging, outcome-led, plain-spoken. Business coaching that talks like a real person who has run a business, not a consultant reading a script. Never hype, never jargon, never corporate. **US English.**

---

## Who we are talking to

Owner-operators of $1M to $10M companies who are stretched, exhausted, and the bottleneck their whole business runs through. They want their time back and they want the business to be worth something without them in every seat. They are smart and practical, and they have heard every empty promise. They respond to plain talk and proof.

So: respect their intelligence, speak to the pain they actually feel (no time, can't step away, it all depends on me), and lead with the outcome.

---

## Voice principles

1. **Lead with the outcome.** Start with the result they get (hours back, profit found), then the how. Never bury the payoff.
2. **Say it straight.** Short, clear sentences. Cut filler. If a sentence is not doing work, delete it.
3. **Warm, on their side.** Encouraging, never preachy or superior. We are in it with them.
4. **Concrete over abstract.** "Five metrics you check in 15 minutes every Monday" beats "a robust performance framework." Specifics build trust.
5. **Proof, not hype.** Real numbers, real outcomes, never invented. The "$50,000 found versus $5,000 price" framing does the persuading; we do not need exclamation marks.
6. **Plain-spoken business talk.** Talk like Matt would across the table, not like a corporate deck. A little casual warmth is good.

---

## The worldview that runs through everything

**Business Without You.** The owner being the single point of failure is the enemy. Every piece of copy should, somewhere, touch the feeling of being trapped in your own business and the relief of getting out of the way. Time back. Profit found. A business that runs without you.

---

## Words and moves we use

- Outcomes first: "15 hours back," "up to $50,000 in profit you didn't know you had," "a business that runs without you."
- Owner-to-owner language: "you're the bottleneck," "it all runs through you," "get out of your own way."
- The signature frame: profit found versus price paid. "We typically find at least $50,000. The program is $5,000. You do the math."
- Plain, confident statements. Short paragraphs. White space.

---

## Words and moves we avoid

- **No em dashes, ever.** House rule, enforced underneath. Use a period, a comma, or "to" for ranges.
- **No hype words:** revolutionary, game-changer, unlock, supercharge, next-level, crush it, 10x, secret sauce.
- **No corporate jargon:** synergy, leverage (as a verb), holistic, ecosystem, robust framework, value-add, circle back, deep dive.
- **No vague promises.** Never say "transform your business" without saying exactly how and to what result.
- **No invented facts, numbers, or testimonials.** If we do not have it, we do not say it.

---

## US English

Matt is American and the audience is American. Use US spelling and usage: optimize, organize, color, behavior, program (not programme), check (not cheque), math (not maths). Do not impose UK spellings on any client-facing copy.

---

## A few quick before / after touches

- Hype: "This will completely revolutionize how you run your business!"
  Us: "You'll get about 15 hours of your week back. Here's exactly how."

- Corporate: "We leverage a holistic framework to optimize organizational performance."
  Us: "We find the one thing holding you back, and we fix it."

- Vague: "Unlock hidden value in your company."
  Us: "We typically find up to $50,000 in profit already sitting in the business. The program costs $5,000."
