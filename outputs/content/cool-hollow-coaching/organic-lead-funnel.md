# Cool Hollow Coaching: Organic Lead Funnel

The funnel architecture behind the Instagram-led, no-ad-spend path to Business Without You. Every piece points to one job: get a stretched owner-operator to the free hidden-profit analyzer, then to a booked call.

---

## The shape of it

```
HOOK (Reels/TikTok, 2x/day)
   |  "you're the bottleneck" pain-point video, 15-45 seconds
   v
BIO LINK
   |  one link, one job: "Find out what's quietly costing you money"
   v
HIDDEN-PROFIT ANALYZER (free tool, lead magnet)
   |  uploads a P&L, gets a real number back, leaves an email to see the full breakdown
   v
NURTURE (DM or email, 1-3 touches)
   |  reinforces the number found, invites a no-pressure call
   v
PROFIT DISCOVERY CALL (booked via GoHighLevel once live)
   |  Mark or Matt, 20-30 min, diagnose + pitch
   v
BUSINESS WITHOUT YOU (12-week program, $5,000)
```

Every stage has exactly one job. The hook's job is a stop and a click, nothing else, it does not need to sell the program. The analyzer's job is to make the $50K promise feel real with the owner's own numbers, not a stranger's claim. The call is the only place an actual sale happens.

---

## Why the hook has to be the pain, not the offer

A scrolling owner-operator is not looking for a coaching program. They're looking for the next thing to put out. The hook has 1-2 seconds to interrupt that and make them feel seen, specifically, not generically. "Business coaching for entrepreneurs" gets scrolled past. "You haven't taken a real day off in three years and you call that running a business" gets watched.

Every hook in this system should pass one test: **could this line only be said to the exact buyer (a $1M-$10M owner who's the bottleneck), or could it apply to anyone?** If it could apply to anyone, it's not sharp enough yet.

## The six pain lanes

Pulled straight from the seven milestones, since the curriculum already maps the real pain points. Rotating across all six keeps the feed from feeling like one repeated pitch and lets different owners self-select into the lane that's actually theirs right now.

| Lane | Milestone source | The wound |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Time and identity | Milestone 1 | "I built a job, not a business" |
| 2. Flying blind | Milestone 3 | No real numbers, find out about problems weeks late |
| 3. Hidden profit | Milestone 4 | Money sitting in the business nobody's found yet |
| 4. Cash fear | Milestone 5 | Can't sleep because of what's three weeks out |
| 5. The ceiling | Milestone 6 | Growth capped at exactly what the owner can personally do |
| 6. Single point of failure | Milestone 7 | The business doesn't survive a two-week vacation, let alone an exit |

Lane 3 (Hidden Profit) gets the heaviest rotation, since it's the one with the most direct line to the free tool, the hook can end on "I built a free tool that finds this in five minutes, link in bio," which is the cleanest possible handoff to the funnel's next stage.

---

## Posting cadence and mix (matches the 2x/day target in strategy.md)

- **1 post a day from Lane 3 (Hidden Profit)**, since it converts most directly to the analyzer.
- **1 post a day rotating through Lanes 1, 2, 4, 5, 6**, so the feed doesn't read as one-note and reaches owners who don't yet think of "profit" as their problem (some think their problem is time, or the ceiling, or the team).
- Every post, regardless of lane, ends on a version of the same CTA: link in bio to the free tool, or "comment PROFIT and I'll send it to you" as a DM-trigger alternative for when the algorithm favors comments over clicks.

---

## Format notes for whoever shoots these

- **Hook delivered to camera, first 3 seconds, no intro, no logo, no "hey guys."** The pain line is the cold open.
- **15-45 seconds total.** Long enough to land the diagnosis, short enough that the algorithm doesn't penalize a high drop-off.
- **Captions burned in.** Most of this audience watches muted at first.
- **One CTA, stated once, near the end, not repeated three times.** Repeating it reads as needy, which cuts against the brand's plain-spoken, credible tone.
