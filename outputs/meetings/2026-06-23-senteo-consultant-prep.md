# Meeting Prep: Business Plan Review with Senteo

**Date:** June 23, 2026
**Who you're meeting:** Owner of Senteo (senteo.net), a business transformation and customer experience consulting firm, 25+ years experience, proprietary tools (CXCI, CEI, Five Promises, Relationship Strength Score), typically works with mid-to-large enterprises in banking and financial services.

**One thing to say out loud early in the meeting:** his usual clients are large enterprises. You're a pre-launch solo operator. Ask him directly how much of his framework still applies at your scale, that question alone will get you better, more honest feedback than pretending you're a bigger business than you are.

---

## Part 1: What We're Trying to Do

### The business
**Cool Hollow Coaching** is the online scale-up arm of **Cool Hollow Solutions**, Mark's existing in-person advisory firm (about 35 clients, ~$60,000/month, capacity-capped because 1:1 advisory doesn't scale past a certain number of clients per consultant).

### The offer
**Business Without You**, a tiered coaching program:
- **Tier 1:** $5,000, 12 weeks, for owner-operators of $1M-$10M companies who are the single point of failure in their own business.
- **Tier 2:** for $10M+ owners, tax strategy, investment, scaling, with a weekly live community. Pricing not yet set.

### The promise
**15 hours back, plus at least $50,000 in profit found.** This is the spine of the whole pitch: the program is positioned as a discovery (you'll find money you didn't know you had), not a cost.

### The honest gap, worth raising with him directly
This $50,000 figure is the curriculum's own benchmark and Mark's track record from 20 years of running his consulting firm personally. It is **not yet proven in this self-administered, productized format** with a real cohort. Ask him how he'd think about a guarantee or headline claim like this before there's case-study proof behind it, this is exactly the kind of credibility question a CX/relationship consultant should have a sharp opinion on.

### The long-term goal
$3M/year, 500 clients/year from Cool Hollow Coaching, system "waxed" by end of July, product live by year end.

## Part 2: How We're Going to Do It

### The curriculum
7 milestones, 26 skills, delivered over 12 weeks (8 teaching weeks, 3 implementation weeks, 1 finale). Current order:

1. Reclaim the Owner's Time and Identity
2. Define the Destination with Precision
3. Install the Dashboard That Tells the Truth
4. Discover the Hidden Profit
5. Build Cash Confidence
6. Break the Binding Constraint
7. Build the Team That Builds the Business

### The delivery model
Mark fronts every module (opens and closes each one), with specialists going deep on their domain: a CFO (Cam) for Milestones 4-5, a hiring/people specialist (Hannah) for Milestone 7. Live Q&A calls stay Mark's domain throughout.

### The tools (this is the differentiator worth highlighting)
Every milestone has a real, working tool behind it, not just a video lesson:
- A free lead-magnet "hidden-profit analyzer" (P&L upload, instant pricing/cost/cash checks)
- Seven in-program tools, one per milestone, each scoring the client's own data and producing a specific, named recommendation (the Profit Discovery Audit, the 13-Week Cash Forecast, the Bottleneck Breakthrough Plan, etc.)

All seven are built and working today, but only run locally, not yet deployed to a public web address.

### What's still genuinely undecided
- The platform: a free/paid/locked Skool community versus building a fully custom coaching platform from scratch
- Final pricing structure for Tier 2
- Logo and visual identity (palette and font direction are drafted, nothing locked)
- Mark's AI voice/face clone, in progress but not functional yet (this is a content-scaling project, not a launch blocker)

## Part 3: The Marketing Funnel So Far

### The structure (planned, not live yet)
One Skool community, three tiers:

| Tier | What it is | What it unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| Free | Anyone joins | Open discussion, 1-2 teaser lessons, the free analyzer tool, locked classroom titles visible (not clickable) |
| Paid community | ~$39-49/month | Live specialist calls, community chat, classroom stays locked |
| Program | $5,000 | The full 12-week classroom, every lesson linked to its working tool |

### The lead-flow plan
- Instagram from scratch, twice-daily posting, hook-first content
- Leaning on Mark's existing network at Cool Hollow Solutions for the first real audience, not starting from pure cold reach
- The free analyzer as the core proof-before-purchase mechanic
- A welcome email sequence, drafted but not yet connected to a live system

### What's actually live right now
- The free hidden-profit analyzer (built, tested, not yet deployed publicly)
- The brand palette and voice (drafted, not locked)
- Zero Instagram followers, zero community members, zero CRM (none of the funnel infrastructure is live yet)

### The real blockers, in order
1. The seven tools need a public web address before Skool can link to them
2. The Skool group itself doesn't exist yet
3. No video content exists yet (teaser lessons, Module 1)
4. GoHighLevel (the planned CRM) hasn't been started

## Part 4: Questions Worth Asking Him

### On his core expertise (customer experience and relationship-building), tailored to what he'd actually have a sharp opinion on
1. "Your work is built around measuring relationship strength and customer experience at enterprise scale. What's the smallest version of that thinking that would actually apply to a free community of a few hundred people, versus a bank's customer base?"
2. "The free-to-paid mechanic here relies on visible-but-locked content creating FOMO. From a customer experience lens, where does that approach typically backfire, what's the tipping point where it feels manipulative instead of motivating?"
3. "You talk about 'Learn, Do, See, Extend' as a methodology. Does a 12-week, video-plus-tool curriculum like ours actually map to that, or are we missing a step?"

### On credibility and proof (the $50,000 claim)
4. "If you were advising a client launching a high-ticket offer with an unproven headline number, how would you stage the claim, soft language now versus a hard guarantee later, or something else entirely?"
5. "What would you want to see before you'd trust a guarantee like 'we'll keep working until we find $50,000'? What's the actual minimum proof bar?"

### On the funnel structure itself
6. "Is the three-tier free/paid/locked model the right shape, or would you structure the journey from stranger to $5,000 client differently?"
7. "Where do most coaching/consulting funnels like this leak the most people, before they ever see the price?"

### On positioning against his own world
8. "Your clients are usually mid-to-large enterprises. What's the biggest mistake a solo operator makes trying to apply enterprise-grade thinking (your tools, frameworks, rigor) to a business this size?"

### A direct, slightly uncomfortable one worth asking
9. "If you were in my position right now, with a built program and no audience yet, what's the one thing you'd fix before doing anything else?"

## How to use this in the meeting

Lead with Part 1 and 2 (what and how) so he understands the offer before judging the funnel. Spend the most real time on Part 4's questions, that's where his specific expertise (not generic startup advice) actually adds value you can't get elsewhere. Write down anything that contradicts what's in this document, that's the most useful kind of feedback to bring back.
