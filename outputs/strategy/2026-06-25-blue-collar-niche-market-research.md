# Market Research: Blue-Collar High-Ticket Coaching

**Date:** 25 June 2026
**Question:** If Cool Hollow Coaching niches down to blue-collar owner-operators, (1) how ripe is the market, (2) does the niche cap the $1.5M+ goal, and (3) will old-school, time-poor owners commit to a program?

All figures below are from public sources, cited. Where a number is an estimate or a range, it says so.

---

## The short version

1. **The market is ripe, proven, and already paying premium.** This is not an empty niche. It is one of the most actively monetized coaching sub-markets in the country.
2. **The niche does not cap $1.5M.** The target pool is hundreds of thousands of firms. You need a few hundred clients a year. And competitors prove you are currently underpriced, so the ceiling is higher, not lower.
3. **Old-school and time-poor is real, but it is a design and channel problem, not a market problem.** The time objection is literally the offer. The fix for engagement is known: short lessons, high price, community, accountability. The channel fix is Facebook and YouTube over Instagram.

---

## 1. How ripe is the market?

**The wider coaching market is large and growing.** The US professional coaching industry is roughly **$16 billion** (ResearchAndMarkets, Dec 2025), reported as having more than doubled since 2016. Other trackers put US business coaching near **$20 billion** in 2025. Either way: big, growing, money flowing.

**The home-services market underneath it is enormous.** The US home-services market is estimated at **$650 billion to $750 billion a year** (BDR, 2025), with HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and the trades growing steadily.

**The trades coaching sub-niche is crowded with real, profitable players.** This is the key finding. Blue-collar owners are not an untested audience. They are already buying high-ticket coaching at scale:

- **Breakthrough Academy** (the clearest comp): a $5,700 setup fee plus $999 / $1,999 / $2,999 a month depending on company size, over a roughly 2-year-9-month program. That is a total of about **$38,700 to $105,300 per client** (btacademy.com pricing page). Their members are exactly your target: trades owners from $500K to $3M+.
- **Tommy Mello / Home Service Freedom**: a low-ticket ladder ($97 course, $199/month group program) feeding higher-priced events and masterminds. Proves the funnel model works in this space.
- **The Contractor Fight (Tom Reber)**: a tiered structure (flagship group program, an advanced annual program for $2M+ companies, and private coaching). Same tiered shape you are planning.

**What this means for you:** demand is validated, willingness to pay is deep, and the tiered model is proven. The risk is not "will they buy." It is "why you over the incumbents." Your wedge is real: almost every player above leads with leads, sales, and revenue growth. Few lead with the **financial side** (hidden profit, cash confidence, a real CFO in the room) and the **owner-exit** angle (a business that runs without you). That is an open lane.

---

## 2. Does the niche cap the $1.5M goal?

**No, and it is not close.**

The addressable pool is vast. As of 2020 there were nearly **990,000 plumbing and HVAC contractor establishments** in the US (Statista, US Census data). IBISWorld 2026 counts roughly **120,000 HVAC contractors** and **129,000 plumbers** as standalone categories, before adding electrical, landscaping, roofing, construction, fleet, and the rest of the blue-collar world. The realistic target universe is in the **hundreds of thousands to low millions** of firms, the large majority of them small.

Now the math. $1.5M at your $5,000 Tier 1 is **300 clients a year**. Blend in Tier 2 at $22,000 and you need far fewer. 300 clients out of hundreds of thousands of firms is a **rounding error of the market**. The niche cannot cap you at this scale.

**The pricing headroom is the bigger point.** Breakthrough Academy charges $38K to $105K for the same buyer you are targeting. Your $5K and $22K are cheap by comparison. That is not a reason to hike prices today (you are pre-launch with no track record), but it tells you the ceiling is well above $1.5M, and that as you build proof you have real room to raise prices and hit the goal with fewer clients.

## 3. Will old-school, time-poor owners commit?

This is the legitimate concern. It splits in two, and both halves are solvable.

**"Old-school / not online."** Partly true, but it is a channel question, not a reachability question. The coaches above built their entire audiences on this group, so these owners clearly are online and do buy. The platform mix matters: **Facebook is where older owners are, and YouTube is the single most-used platform in the US** (multiple 2025 marketing sources). Instagram and TikTok skew younger and more visual. For reaching blue-collar **owners** (not homeowners), the read is **Facebook and YouTube first, Instagram second.** Worth testing rather than assuming Instagram is the front door.

**"No time to commit."** This is your strongest selling angle, not a weakness. Your whole promise is Business Without You, 15 hours back. An owner saying "I have no time for a course" is telling you he is the exact person who needs it. Say it back to him.

But the engagement risk is real, and the fix is well documented:

- **Short lessons win.** The data is consistent: lessons should be **30 minutes or less, ideally 5 to 15 minutes** per specific topic, with content dripped in small pieces. (Note: this directly challenges the 30 to 75 minute lesson length in the current deck. Shorter is better for this audience. Worth revisiting.)
- **High price helps completion.** A higher price makes people show up and finish, because they want their money's worth. Your $5K works in your favor here.
- **Community and accountability raise completion.** Live calls, a community, and someone checking in are the difference between a finished program and a refund. Your recorded-plus-live model already fits.
- **Done-with-you, not homework-heavy.** The tools should do the analysis for the owner, not hand him a pile of work. Your seven milestone tools already do this.

---

## What I would do with this

1. **Keep the niche.** It is proven, premium, and does not cap the goal. The data backs the decision.
2. **Sharpen the wedge** to finance plus owner-exit, since that is the open lane versus the leads-and-sales incumbents.
3. **Rethink the front-door channel** toward Facebook and YouTube, not Instagram-first.
4. **Reconsider lesson length.** 30 to 75 minutes may be too long for this time-poor audience. The engagement data favors short.
5. **Lean into the time objection** in the messaging instead of working around it.

## Competitor deep-dive: pricing, what they sell, and where they lean

Four of the biggest names in trades and home-service coaching, with real pricing.

| Competitor | Price | What they actually sell | Commitment | The lean |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Breakthrough Academy** | $5,700 setup + $999 to $2,999 a month (about **$38,700 to $105,300 total**) | Operating systems and implementation, leadership, scaling | ~2 years 9 months | Build the systems so the business runs |
| **The Contractor Fight (Battleground)** | **$697 a month** or **$6,970 a year** | Sales, pricing confidence, mindset, knowing your numbers | Monthly, ongoing | Toughen up, charge more, sell better |
| **Tommy Mello / Home Service Freedom** | $97 course, $199/mo group, $10K to $30K consulting add-ons, paid events | Scaling to big, the marketing machine, acquisitions | Low-ticket ladder up to high-ticket | Go huge, build an empire |

**Cool Hollow Coaching, for comparison:** $5,000 for a 12-week program (Tier 1), $22,000 a year (Tier 2). Promise: 15 hours back, up to $50,000 in profit found, a business that runs without you.

### What they all have in common (and what they miss)

Read the "lean" column. Every one of them sells some version of the same thing: **grow, scale, sell more, build bigger.** Breakthrough Academy builds systems. Contractor Fight sharpens sales and mindset. Mello sells the path to an empire. CertainPath sells enterprise systems and software.

**Almost none of them lead with the owner getting their life back.** They lead with the business getting bigger. That is the open lane.

They also share three structural costs that work in your favor:
- **Long and expensive.** BTA locks owners in for nearly three years at up to six figures. CertainPath and Contractor Fight are open-ended monthly commitments. There is no clean, finish-it-in-12-weeks, get-a-result-and-go option for a time-poor owner who is not ready to marry a coaching company.
- **Coaching-led, not tool-led.** They sell access to coaches and training libraries. The owner still has to do the thinking. Few hand the owner a tool that does the analysis and outputs the answer.
- **Personality or systems brands, not finance.** Their credibility is a founder's story (Mello, Reber) or a systems methodology (BTA, CertainPath). None of them put a real CFO in the room whose job is to find hidden profit and build a cash forecast.

## The gap, and your strength in the offer

### The gap (the open lane nobody owns)

**"Get your time back and get out," led by a real CFO, in a short, tool-driven program.** Spelled out:

1. **Owner-exit over owner-growth.** Everyone sells "scale your business." You sell "build a business that runs without you and get 15 hours of your week back." Same buyer, opposite emotional promise. For an exhausted 55-hour-a-week owner, time back beats bigger.
2. **Finance depth from an actual CFO.** Hidden profit, cash confidence, a 13-week forecast, $50,000 found. Competitors teach "know your numbers" at a surface level. None field a CFO who finds the money in the books. Cam is a real, differentiated asset here.
3. **Low commitment as the front door.** 12 weeks and $5,000, not 3 years and $100,000. That is the on-ramp the market is missing for the owner who will not sign a multi-year deal. Tier 2 at $22,000 is where the bigger, longer relationship lives, once trust is earned.
4. **Tools that do the work.** Seven milestone tools that take the owner's own numbers and output the answer. For a time-poor, old-school owner, "upload your P&L and we show you the money" beats "watch 1,000 videos."

### Your strengths to lead with

- **The promise is time, not scale.** 15 hours back plus up to $50,000 found is concrete, outcome-led, and aimed at the pain the owner actually feels, not the ambition a guru assumes he has.
- **A real operating firm behind it.** Cool Hollow Solutions runs about 45 clients at roughly $95,000 a month. You are operators with a track record, not a personality brand selling a course.
- **A CFO in the room.** The finance angle is the single most defensible, hardest-to-copy part of the offer.
- **Done-with-you and time-light.** The tools plus short, recorded lessons plus live accountability fit the time-poor owner the competitors quietly make work hard.
- **Priced as the easy yes.** $5,000 against a market that charges $38,000 to $105,000 is not a discount to apologize for. It is a low-risk first step that the incumbents structurally cannot offer without cannibalizing their own high-ticket programs.

### Where you are honestly weaker, and what to do about it

- **You are unknown.** They have thousands of reviews, big audiences, years of momentum. Fix: borrow Cool Hollow Solutions' credibility and real client stories from day one, and build the story bank.
- **No blue-collar track record yet (to confirm).** Your wins need to be real trades-owner wins to land in this niche. This is exactly why the story bank with Mark and Cam matters, and why the $6.7M win needs confirming before it is used.
- **No content library, community, or events yet.** They have all three. Fix: the tools and the finance angle are your wedge while you build the rest. Do not try to out-library CertainPath. Win on the gap, not on volume.

### The one-line positioning this points to

> The other guys help you scale. We help you step back. The same team that runs a real advisory firm, plus a CFO who finds the profit hiding in your books, get you 15 hours a week back and a business that runs without you. In 12 weeks, not 3 years.
