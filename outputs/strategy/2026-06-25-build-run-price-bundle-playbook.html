Part 1: How we build it (one-time, done once)

This is the heavy lifting, and it only happens once. After this, every new client is a quick clone.

  1. Open the GoHighLevel account. Start on the $97 plan for the pilot. One account holds every client.
  2. Build one master setup. Inside it: the instant missed-call text, the qualifying questions, the calendar booking, the quote-chaser, and the weekly recovered-money recap. This is where the real work is.
  3. Test it to death on your own phone and a dummy number until it's flawless.
  4. Save it as a snapshot. A snapshot is a frozen copy of the whole setup. From here on, a new client is "apply snapshot, fill in their details," not "build from scratch."

That's the build. It's a one-time job, done by your builder, with you watching so you understand it.

Part 2: How we run it for a client (repeatable, the part that scales)

Every new client follows the same short path. This is the bit that becomes the business.

  1. Onboard (10 minutes with the owner). Collect: business name, owner name, trade, area served, business hours, and the phone number they want it on. One short form or a quick call.
  2. Clone their account from the snapshot. Two minutes.
  3. Fill in their blanks. Their name and trade drop into the text templates automatically. Every text now sounds like them.
  4. Connect their phone and calendar. Either we give them a fresh business number, or we forward their existing line so missed calls flow into the system. A 2-minute step we walk them through.
  5. Register them for business texting (A2P). A one-time compliance form. We handle it.
  6. Go live and send a test. They miss a test call, they watch the text fire, they get it. That moment sells the whole thing.
  7. Then it runs itself. The owner does nothing. The weekly recap auto-sends. We monitor and handle any "can you change this word" requests.

Time per client after the build: about 15 to 20 minutes in the pilot, dropping to near-zero once SaaS mode auto-onboards them.

Part 3: Who does what (and the honest scaling note)

  • The one-time build (snapshot, automations, SaaS setup): your builder.
  • Selling it and onboarding owners: you. This is the owner-facing, relationship part, your strength.
  • Day-to-day running: light. Monitoring and the odd change request.
  • At real volume (100+ clients): a part-time support person or VA handles onboarding admin, texting-registration hiccups, and owner questions. Small, known cost of scale. Not you at midnight, and not needed for a long while.

The three stages:

  • Pilot: $97 plan, build it, run it by hand on 1 to 3 friendly owners, capture the first real "money recovered" numbers.
  • Productize: move to the $497 SaaS plan, turn on auto-onboarding and auto-billing.
  • Scale: sell it as the front door to the coaching, feed every saved job into the story bank.

Part 5: How it sits with the coaching (the offer ladder)

This tool is not separate from Business Without You. It's the bottom rung of a ladder that walks an owner up to the full program.

  1. Free taster (top of funnel). A guide or the hidden-profit analyzer. Costs them nothing, proves you know your stuff.
  2. Never Miss a Job ($149/mo). The low-risk first "yes." It recovers real money and builds trust. This is the wedge.
  3. Business Without You, Tier 1 ($5,000, 12 weeks). Once an owner has felt you put money back in his pocket, the program is an easy step up. The tool literally demonstrated milestone one (get your time and your leads out of your own hands).
  4. Tier 2 ($25,000 to $35,000 a year). The owners who want the whole team and the long game.

Each rung makes the next one easier. The tool earns the right to sell the program.

Inside the program: Never Miss a Job is included and runs free for the 12 weeks of Tier 1. After the program, it converts to the $149/mo to keep running. That gives you a recurring revenue tail from every client, long after the 12 weeks end.
