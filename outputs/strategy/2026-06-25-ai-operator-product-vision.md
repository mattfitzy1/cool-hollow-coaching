## 1. The big idea (and why your instinct is right)

You want to build one product that does two things for a trades owner:

1. **Gets and keeps customers** (the lead engine).
2. **Runs the money** (the finance brain), so the owner can fire the bookkeeper-in-his-head and get real reports on his phone in minutes.

Here is why this is the right instinct: **the competitors only do one side.** Scout does operations and leads. profitabletradie sells AI tools. Nobody is pairing a lead engine with a real finance brain for blue-collar owners. The finance angle is the exact open lane the earlier research found. So this isn't scope creep for its own sake, it's aiming straight at the gap nobody owns.

**The one-line product:** *"Get more jobs and finally understand your money, done for you, and it runs itself."*

---

## 2. What to copy from Scout AI (the model, not the features)

Scout's product is ordinary. Their **business model** is sharp. Copy these four moves exactly:

- **Pay on results.** "You don't pay until it's running and delivering." This is the easiest yes in the world and it's the same model we already chose for Database Reactivation. Lead with it.
- **Scarcity.** "Only 6 onboards a month." Real or chosen, it makes people act.
- **Live in a week.** Speed is a selling point. Owners expect tech to be slow and painful.
- **Cancel anytime.** Removes the last objection.

These four are free to copy and they work. Bolt them onto your offer.

## 3. The finance brain: what to keep, what to cut

You listed a big pile of finance and ops skills. Here is the honest filter, because about half of them are built for Wall Street, not for a $2M HVAC shop. Bolting equity-research tools onto a plumber's phone would confuse the owner and dilute the product.

**KEEP (these are what a trades owner actually asks):**

| Owner's real question | The feature | Maps to skill |
|---|---|---|
| "Am I actually making money?" | Profit / hidden-profit analysis | small-business margin-analyzer, the P&L analyzer |
| "How did this month go?" | Month-end summary | small-business close-month / month-end-prep |
| "Can I make payroll?" | Payroll planner | small-business plan-payroll |
| "What does my cash look like?" | Cash-flow snapshot + 13-week forecast | small-business cash-flow-snapshot |
| "Am I charging enough?" | Price / margin check | small-business price-check |
| "What do I owe in tax, am I ready?" | Tax summary | small-business tax-prep |
| "What's my business worth?" | Simple valuation | a simplified valuation, owners love this one |

**CUT (built for public companies and M&A, not trades owners):**

- **Equity teaser** — that's an investment-banking document for selling a company to private equity. Not a day-to-day owner tool.
- **Earnings analysis** — that's for public companies reporting quarterly. A private trades business has no earnings call.
- **Full merger / business-valuation-for-merger model** — a simple "what's it worth" is great, a full merger model is overkill.
- **smb router, smb onboard** — these are internal routing tools, not customer-facing finance.

**The keeper list is the product:** upload your numbers, get a plain-English answer to the seven questions above, on your phone, in minutes. That is a CFO in your pocket. That is the moat.

## 4. The full product, in two halves

**Half A — The Lead Engine (gets and keeps customers):**
- Never Miss a Job (missed-call rescue)
- Database Reactivation (wake up old customers)
- Lead-gap agent (catch leads slipping through web forms, DMs, etc.)
- Follow-up agent (chase quotes and quiet leads)
- Prospect booking (self-serve calendar)

**Half B — The Finance Brain (runs the money):**
- The seven keepers from section 3, fed by uploaded and logged data, output as plain-English reports on the owner's phone.

Together: *the AI operator that gets you jobs and runs your books, so the business runs without you.* That sentence is your whole brand.

## 5. The honest build reality (read this before you fall in love)

Not all of this is equally easy, and I'd be failing you to pretend it is.

- **The Lead Engine is buildable now.** It runs on GoHighLevel, the way we already specced. Weeks, cheap, real money fast. This is the part that pays your December debt.
- **The Finance Brain is a real software product.** "Upload your data, get CFO reports on your phone" is not a weekend config. It's a genuine app: data upload, parsing, analysis, a clean mobile output. It's months of build and real money, and it needs to be accurate, because wrong numbers in a finance tool is the one mistake you can't make. Start it small (the P&L profit analyzer, already on the roadmap) and add one report at a time.

**So the sequence is not "build it all." It's:**

1. **Phase 1, now (cash):** the Lead Engine, sold pay-on-results like Scout. Pays the debt, proves you can deliver.
2. **Phase 2 (the wedge):** the profit analyzer plus a simple money dashboard (profit, cash, payroll, price). One upload, plain answers.
3. **Phase 3 (the moat):** the fuller CFO-in-your-pocket (month-end, tax summary, valuation).

You sell the dream (the AI operator that runs your business) and you deliver it in stages. The lead engine earns the trust and the cash that funds the finance brain.
