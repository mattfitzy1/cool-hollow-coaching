1. Positioning: the foundation everything hangs off

Positioning is who we're for, what we promise, and why us instead of anyone else. Get this right and the content, the funnel, and the ads all write themselves. Get it wrong and no amount of marketing spend saves it.

Who we're for

Blue-collar owner-operators, $500k to $10M in revenue, three or more employees. Trades, contractors, services, the businesses Cool Hollow already serves and already has wins in.

Why niche instead of "any business owner":

  • Specificity wins the scroll. A contractor stops on "for owners still stuck running every job" and scrolls past "for business owners." Being seen is what earns the follow and eventually the sale.
  • The market is plenty big. We need 3 to 5 clients a month. There are hundreds of thousands of blue-collar businesses in this revenue band in the US. Market size doesn't bind us for years.
  • Niche is a starting point, not a cage. We own the blue-collar lane first, build the case studies, then expand to adjacent owners from strength. Narrowing now is what lets us broaden later.
  • It's where all our ammunition is. Every real win we can tell is from this world. Going broad throws away the one thing no other coach can copy: we've actually done this, for these exact people, with receipts.
Open decision for Cam: broad blue-collar, or name the two or three specific trades where Cool Hollow has the deepest track record? Tighter is more powerful, but only if the proof is there. Worth a 10-minute conversation.

The promise (and why the old hook needs work)

Sam was right that "find $50k in profit" is too weak on its own, and here's the precise reason: it's a *claim*, not *proof*. "We find $50k" sounds like every other coach's billboard. The number isn't the problem. The missing story is.

The deeper truth about this owner: his real pain isn't money, it's time and being trapped. He can't take a vacation. The business can't run a day without him. So we lead with the life and prove it with money.

The layered hook:

  • Emotional lead (the hook): a business that runs without you. Mark runs his remotely. You can too.
  • The proof (the close): specific, real, named client wins. "Here's the whiteboard of how we saved an owner $6.7M in capital gains tax." Receipts, not adjectives.

Same outcomes we always believed in. We just stop *claiming* them and start *showing* them.

The positioning statement (our north star)

For blue-collar owners who are the bottleneck in their own business, Cool Hollow Coaching is the program that installs the systems to get you out of the day-to-day, proven by a team that has actually done it for owners like you, not a guru selling a dream.

Everything downstream inherits from that sentence.

3. The story bank: our unfair advantage, made repeatable

This is the piece an agency can't build for us, because they don't have our clients. The content engine runs on stories, so first we build the inventory.

The framework for a sellable client story (5 parts):

  1. The owner before. Who they were, how stuck. ("A roofing contractor working 70-hour weeks, hadn't taken a vacation in four years.")
  2. The one thing we found. The specific leak, constraint, or hidden profit.
  3. The move. What we changed. Plain and concrete.
  4. The result. The number and the life change. ("$214k he was leaving on the table, and his first week off in four years.")
  5. The lesson. The takeaway any owner watching can use today. This is what makes it content, not a brag.

The build task: sit with Mark and Cam and pull 10 to 15 of these from real Cool Hollow Solutions clients. Get the numbers right, get permission where needed, never invent a single figure. That story bank becomes months of content, the spine of the VSL, and the proof on every sales call.

The $6.7M capital gains story from the call is story number one. We need 10 more like it.

4. The content engine: a media company that happens to sell coaching

Sam's best line, and the whole strategy in one sentence. We don't make ads. We build an audience. The selling happens because the audience trusts us, not because we shouted.

What we replace, and why

The current agency's content is generic and AI-flavored, and it doesn't generate leads because it sounds like everyone else. The fix isn't better production. It's authenticity: Mark, a whiteboard, a real story, told straight. Lower gloss, higher trust.

The content system

Three formats, on repeat:

  1. Whiteboard breakdowns. Mark walks through a real client win on a whiteboard. Our highest-trust format. This is the flagship.
  2. Owner-truth shorts. 30 to 60 seconds naming a pain this owner feels today ("if your business can't run a week without you, you don't own a business, you own a job"). Built for the scroll.
  3. Behind-the-business. Mark actually running things remotely. The promise, shown not told.

Cadence: start at a sustainable pace we can hold for months, not a sprint we abandon in three weeks. Consistency beats volume. Two to three pieces a week of *real* content beats two a day of filler.

The hook discipline: every piece earns the first three seconds. We research what's working across the best blue-collar and business creators, adapt the *structure* of their hooks (never copy the content), and lead with the owner's pain or a number.

On the AI-Mark question: filming everything live with Mark is the bottleneck. An AI clone (likeness and voice) is the unlock for volume, and it stays a live priority. But note: it's in tension with "authentic, low-gloss" content. The play is to use real Mark for the high-trust whiteboard flagship pieces and reserve any AI clone for scale formats once the voice quality is genuinely there. We don't fake the trust-builders.

Brand clarity (remove the friction)

A cold owner landing on our page should understand in five seconds: who this is for, what they get, and what to do next. No cleverness, no cognitive tax. One clear value proposition, one clear next step.

The model is simple and it's the right one: organic warms, paid amplifies, the system converts.

Why this order matters: we don't run cold paid ads to a $5k offer from a brand nobody knows. That's how you burn money. We build the warm audience organically first, prove the message converts, *then* put paid spend behind the exact content that's already working. Paid is a fuel pump, not an engine.

The lead magnet: the hidden-profit analyzer is the ideal front door. An owner uploads a P&L, sees real leakage and hidden profit, and gets a taste of the promise before paying a cent. It proves the core claim and captures the lead in one move.

6. Ops and automation: GoHighLevel as the backbone

Every lead the funnel generates has to land somewhere and get followed up, automatically. That's GoHighLevel's job.

What it runs:

  • CRM: every lead, tagged by source and stage, kept fully separate from Cool Hollow Solutions' CRM so commission-only reps can work the coaching side cleanly.
  • Booking: discovery and coaching calls scheduled without back-and-forth.
  • Nurture automation: the email and SMS sequences that keep a warm lead warm until they're ready.
  • Pipeline visibility: Matt and Cam can see exactly how many leads are at each stage, so we manage the funnel by numbers, not by gut.

Important guardrail: automation handles the *plumbing* (tagging, scheduling, reminders). It does not message owners pretending to be a person. Anything that reaches a human reads as coming from us, and we see it first.
