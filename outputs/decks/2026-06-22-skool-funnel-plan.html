Why It Converts
05 / 08

Free members see what they're missing, on purpose.

The upgrade pressure is built into the platform, not a sales pitch.

The classroom shows locked titles to free members. They see all seven module names with a lock icon, not just a vague "upgrade" message.
Live calls sit on the shared calendar, visible to everyone. Free members watch a call happening they can't click into, every single week.
One group, not three separate communities. Upgrading feels like unlocking a door already in the room, not joining somewhere new.
Wins get shared publicly in the free feed. A real member's result (with their permission) is worth more than any ad copy we could write.
Before This Goes Live
06 / 08

Three real gaps, nothing theoretical.

Being straight about what's actually standing between this plan and a live funnel.

The seven tools need a public web address. They only run locally today. Skool can't embed or link to something that isn't reachable on the internet. This is the one real technical gate, and it's a known, free fix (e.g. Streamlit Community Cloud).
The teaser and Module 1 videos need filming. Mark on camera directly, or the AI avatar clone once it's ready, whichever is faster. Don't wait on the clone to start.
The $50,000 guarantee language needs to stay a working guarantee, not an absolute. Already softened across the analyzer and the deck. Real proof comes from the first cohort, not before.
