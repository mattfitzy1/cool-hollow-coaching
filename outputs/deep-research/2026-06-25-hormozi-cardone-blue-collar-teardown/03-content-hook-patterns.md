# Content and Hook Patterns: Hormozi, Cardone/Dawson, and a Blue-Collar Exemplar

Research angle 03 of the Hormozi/Cardone blue-collar teardown.
Date: 2026-06-25. Audience target: blue-collar owner-operators ($500K to $10M), trades and services.
Voice for all adaptations: direct, warm, outcome-led, plain-spoken, proof not hype, US English, no em dashes.

---

## Executive Summary

The single most useful thing this research turned up was not Hormozi or Cardone. It was a reel from **Matt Murray, "Blue Collar King" (@therealbluecollarking)**, a real 22-year HVAC operator who runs an 8-figure HVAC company and now coaches trades owners. His content is the Hormozi structure poured into our exact audience and our exact promise ("a business that runs without you"). He is the closest live competitor and the cleanest template to copy. I pulled his reel's real metrics and full script and broke it down beat by beat below.

Three headline takeaways:

1. **The structure that works for our audience is not the money-flex.** It is the operator-to-operator confession plus a specific number plus a "here is what nobody taught you" reframe. Hormozi's "experience hook" ("I've done X, here's what I learned") and his pain-point open both translate cleanly. Cardone's aspirational 10X money-status content mostly does NOT translate, and will likely repel a practical contractor. Brandon Dawson's "I scaled a real company and sold it" authority hook DOES translate, because it is proof, not flash.

2. **Lead with the specific number and the practical pain, not the dream.** The winning blue-collar hook in the wild was "If you own a $2M-$5M HVAC company but you're only making around $100K, there's a reason why." That is a number a contractor recognizes in his own bank account. Our equivalent: "15 hours back" and "up to $50K in profit you already have." Pull the dream out of the abstract and into a figure on a P&L.

3. **The Hormozi look (yellow captions, zoom cuts, sound effects) is now saturated and starting to fatigue.** For a brand-new account in a practical trade, the safer bet in 2026 is plain talking-head, clean captions, real worksite or truck backdrop, and proof. Over-produced "guru" polish reads as exactly the thing a contractor distrusts.

### The hook line (verbatim, first lines of the script)

> "If you own a $2M-$5M HVAC company but you're only making around $100K... There's a reason why. And it's not the market. It's not your overhead."

This is the whole game in three sentences. Note what it does not say. It does not say "scale to 8 figures," it does not flash a car, it does not promise riches. It names a number the owner sees in his own life (a multi-million revenue business that somehow only nets him six figures) and it immediately removes the excuses he has been telling himself (the market, overhead). That removal is the hook's hidden engine: it tells him the problem is solvable and it is not his fault yet.

### Structure, beat by beat

1. **Callout + specific-number pain (0-4s).** "If you own a $2M-$5M HVAC company but you're only making around $100K..." Audience callout fused with a painfully precise financial number. This is Hormozi's "problem-aware, pain-driven" hook done in a trades dialect.
2. **The "it's not what you think" reframe (4-10s).** "And it's not the market. It's not your overhead." Kills the owner's default excuses, which keeps him watching because his current explanation just got taken away.
3. **The identity reframe, the emotional core (10-18s).** "You learned how to be a great technician. No one showed you how to be a great business owner." This is the line that makes a contractor stop scrolling. It reframes his struggle as a gap in training, not a personal failing. It is warm and it is true.
4. **The framework number / open loop (18-30s).** "Here's what nobody taught you about scaling HVAC: There's 3 hidden ceilings holding you back. It's not sales. It's not marketing. It's not about getting more technicians." A numbered framework ("3 hidden ceilings") plus three more excuse-removals. The number creates a saveable, finite promise.
5. **The mindset gut-punch (30-38s).** "You've got a $10M opportunity and a $1M mindset. And hustling harder isn't going to fix it." Names the cap as internal, and removes the only lever the owner thinks he has (work harder).
6. **The authority stack (38-52s).** "I'm Matt Murray. I've been an HVAC business owner for over 22 years. I own multiple commercial and residential HVAC companies. AND, they run without me." Proof, proof, proof, then the exact promise. "They run without me" is the payoff line and it is identical to our flagship promise.
7. **The differentiator (52-60s).** "Other coaches teach from theory and certificates... I teach from 22 years of being an HVAC business owner." Positions against the very guru style he is using, which is smart for a skeptical trades audience.
8. **The CTA (60-66s).** A free HVAC Scaling Workshop with a register link. Soft, value-first, no hard sell, no price mentioned.

### Why it works

- **Specificity is the credibility.** "$2M-$5M," "around $100K," "22 years," "3 hidden ceilings." A contractor's BS detector is calibrated for vagueness. Numbers disarm it.
- **It attacks the excuse, not the person.** Every beat removes a thing the owner blames (market, overhead, sales, marketing, more techs, working harder) until the only thing left is the one Matt sells: how to be an owner, not a technician.
- **The promise IS our promise.** "They run without me" is the literal Business Without You line, validated as a hook in our exact market.
- **The authority is operator authority, not money authority.** He proves he did the actual job for 22 years. He explicitly rejects "theory and certificates." That is the correct authority frame for blue-collar, and it is the opposite of a money-flex.
- **The CTA is a free workshop, not a buy button.** Value-first, which fits a pre-launch, no-advertising-the-unbuilt-product posture.

### The borrowable lesson

Copy this skeleton wholesale, swap HVAC for "your trade" and swap his ceilings for our seven milestones:

> [Specific revenue band] + [painfully specific low take-home] + "there's a reason, and it's not [excuse], not [excuse]" + "you learned to be a great [tradesman], nobody taught you to be a great owner" + "[number] things are holding you back" + "I'm [name], I [operator proof], and my business runs without me" + free value CTA.

## Key Findings: Each Creator's Content System

### Alex Hormozi

**Claim 1: Hormozi's core engine is "Hook, Retain, Reward" with value given away upfront.**
Evidence: Multiple independent breakdowns describe the same three-part model: a bold hook (statement, stat, or pain-point question), retention via story and open loops, and reward by giving real value away free rather than teasing it for later. (itsmostly.com, powercademy.com, opus.pro)
Confidence: **High** (consistent across 5+ sources).

**Claim 2: His signature hook is the "experience / How I" hook, not "How to."**
Evidence: "Use 'How I' rather than 'How to' to establish authority." Example archetypes: "I've built 6 companies to $100M+, here's what nobody tells you" and "I've hired 4,000 people, here are the 3 questions I ask every time." Named variants in the swipe files: Experience Hook, Contrast Hook ("Broke people think X, millionaires think Y"), Secret Hook ("The $100K sales script they don't want you to know"), Confession Hook ("I made this $2M mistake so you don't have to"). (opus.pro, davidschwertfeger.com, reap.video)
Confidence: **High.**

**Claim 3: The "give a framework number" list hook is real and central.**
Evidence: His value equation (Value = Dream Outcome x Likelihood / Time x Effort) drives a list structure that promises a finite, saveable payoff. Real example hooks: "90% of businesses fail, here's why," "I made $100K from one video, this is how." The numbered list ("here are the 3...") raises perceived likelihood and lowers perceived effort, the two numerator/denominator levers. (davidschwertfeger.com, reap.video)
Confidence: **High.**

**Claim 4: Format is solo talking-head, captions-first, because half the audience watches muted.**
Evidence: "50% of people consuming content on TikTok and Instagram reels are not actually listening with audio on," so text overlays carry the message; bold/colored keywords, emojis, zoom cuts, sound effects. Story-based content reportedly gets ~24% more views; animated captions reportedly +42% views (vendor-sourced stats, treat as directional). (opus.pro, riverside.com)
Confidence: **Medium-High** (structure is solid; the exact percentage stats are vendor marketing).

**Claim 5 (contradiction): the Hormozi look is saturating and starting to fatigue in 2026.**
Evidence: "In the last six months there has been a noticeable shift away from this style as the pendulum swings back to organic, authentic, raw content." Every young creator using "the exact same font, colors, and sound effects" prompts the open question "Is the Hormozi style dead?" Small accounts overusing the formula hit diminishing returns. (nowbam.com, joyspace.ai)
Confidence: **Medium-High.** This is the most important caveat for us: borrow the structure, not the costume.

### Grant Cardone

**Claim: Cardone's content is aspirational/aggressive money-and-status 10X content, optimized for shareability over practical value.**
Evidence: His own social advice is "post information that others will share" and "it takes creativity to go viral," with the brand built on "10X your business and life." The aesthetic is wealth, jets, real estate, high-volume motivation. (grantcardone.com)
Confidence: **Medium** (his thesis is well documented; I did not pull individual reel metrics because the tools to do so were unavailable).
Relevance to us: **Low and risky.** The money-flex is the single most likely thing to repel a practical contractor. See "What to avoid."

### Brandon Dawson (Cardone Ventures)

**Claim: Dawson's hook is operator-proof authority and a precise exit number, aimed at small-to-mid business owners. This is the Cardone-world content that DOES translate.**
Evidence: He leads with "30+ years helping owners scale," "sold Audigy Group for $151M at 77X EBITDA," "youngest to ring the American Stock Exchange bell at 29," "10,500+ businesses impacted," "185+ workshops." His framing: scaling is achievable through proven systems and mentorship, "not luck." He explicitly admits he "built businesses the wrong way" first, then learned the right way, which is a confession/credibility move. (bdawson.com/about, theceomagazine.com, cardoneventures.com)
Confidence: **High** on the messaging; the "77X EBITDA" and exit number are repeated verbatim across his own properties.
Relevance to us: **Medium-High.** The "I built and SOLD a real company, here's the system" authority hook and the "I did it the wrong way first" confession both port directly to Mark's positioning. The specific-exit-number move is borrowable. The billion-dollar scale flex is not (too big to be relatable to a $1M trades owner).

## Format and Cadence Recommendation

**Cadence.** The CLAUDE.md target is two posts a day on a new Instagram account. The 2026 algorithm research says the realistic sweet spot for most brands is 3-5 feed/Reel posts a week plus Stories, and that "quality consistency matters more than raw frequency." Recommendation: do not chase two polished Reels a day from a standing start. Run **1 Reel + 1 lighter post (carousel or single still or Story) per day**, where the Reel is the heavy lift and the second slot is repurposed value. That hits the two-a-day cadence without burning out, and protects quality, which is the real ranking driver.

**Format mix (weekly).**
- **3-4 talking-head Reels** (the BCK/Hormozi structure: specific-number hook, excuse removal, identity reframe, one piece of real value, soft CTA). This is the growth engine.
- **2-3 carousels.** Carousels are the save-and-share machine. Use them for "the 5 Monday numbers," "the 7 milestones to a business that runs without you," "3 places hidden profit hides on a contractor's P&L." Save is the highest-intent signal for a business-owner audience because it means "I'll act on this later."
- **1-2 stills or Stories** for proof, behind-the-scenes, or a single sharp quote.

**What drives saves and shares for business owners specifically.** Saveable beats shareable for this audience. Owners save practical, finite, do-it-later assets: checklists, numbered frameworks, a single worksheet, a "test." They share things that make them look smart or that name their pain exactly. So every carousel should be a thing they could screenshot and act on Monday, and every Reel hook should name a number they recognize from their own books.

**Production note for blue-collar specifically.** Given the 2026 fatigue with the polished guru look, shoot plainer than Hormozi. Real backdrop (a truck, a shop, a job site, a desk with actual paperwork), clean readable captions, minimal zoom-cut gimmickry, no flashy sound effects. The aesthetic itself should signal "operator, not guru." Captions on screen are still mandatory because most viewing is muted, but keep them simple, not the rainbow-keyword style.

## What to Avoid for Blue-Collar

1. **The money-and-status flex.** Cardone-style jets, supercars, cash fans, "10X your money" energy. A practical contractor reads this as a salesman, and it actively destroys trust. Our proof is a P&L line and a freed-up Friday, not a lifestyle.
2. **Theory and credentials as authority.** Matt Murray wins by rejecting "theory and certificates." Never lead with frameworks-for-frameworks'-sake or coaching jargon. Lead with "here's what actually happened in a real business."
3. **Over-produced guru polish.** The saturated Hormozi costume (specific yellow font, identical sound effects, rapid zoom cuts) now signals "another online guru." For a new trades account it is a liability, not an asset. Borrow the structure, drop the costume.
4. **Hype words and vague dreams.** "Crush it," "10X," "next level," "unlock," "game-changer." Replace every dream with a number: 15 hours, $50K, 5 numbers, 13 weeks, a Friday off.
5. **Hard-sell CTAs on a pre-launch product.** Per our own rules (no advertising the unbuilt product), CTAs stay value-first: a free workshop, a free test, "send me X and I'll show you," not "buy now" or "join the waitlist for a thing that isn't built."
6. **Inventing proof.** No fake exit numbers, no fake client results, no fake testimonials. Every number in a hook must be real or clearly framed as a hypothetical example.
7. **Talking down.** The audience is smart, busy, and skilled. The identity reframe ("nobody taught you the business side") works because it is respectful: it credits their trade skill and names a real gap. Condescension kills it.
