# Beta Test Subject 2: Bramblewood Creative

A second, deliberately different business shape from Harbor Fleet Maintenance (Beta Test 1), to check whether the milestone tools, and the profit-discovery-audit fix just made, hold up outside the low-margin trades case that surfaced the first round of findings.

---

## The business

**Bramblewood Creative** — branding and marketing agency, Austin, TX. Retainer-based brand and marketing work for mid-size companies, plus project work (rebrands, campaign launches).

- **Owner:** Erin Vasquez, founded the agency 6 years ago.
- **Revenue:** ~$2.4M/year, naturally high-margin (people and software, not cost of goods).
- **Team:** 3 strategists, 4 designers, 2 project managers, 1 ops/bookkeeper, 1 biz dev, Erin.
- **Customers:** 8 retainer clients averaging $18K/month combined make up about 60% of revenue. The 3 largest retainers (45% of total revenue) deal with Erin directly and nobody else. The rest is project-based work sold by the biz dev hire.

## Why Erin is the single point of failure

- Personally runs every new client onboarding call and sets every retainer price herself, no pricing framework exists.
- Reviews and approves every creative deliverable before a client sees it, regardless of which designer made it.
- Is the sole point of contact for the three largest retainer clients; nobody else on the team has the relationship.
- Still does new business strategy calls herself instead of handing them to biz dev.
- Approves every software/tool purchase request from the design team personally, but has never audited what's actually being paid for.
- Works about 58 hours a week, most of it in client-facing review and approval, not strategy.

## Where it hurts

- **Margin looks healthy on paper** (gross margin running around 58%) but Erin suspects, without measuring it, that the smallest retainer clients cost more in her own review time than they're worth.
- **Subscription bloat:** the design team has accumulated a stack of software tools over six years, several barely used, all auto-renewing, nobody has audited the list in two years.
- **One retainer is shrinking:** a mid-size client cut their monthly retainer hours two months ago after a contact left, revenue from that account is visibly declining month over month, unaddressed.
- **No real bench on strategy:** if Erin is out, none of the three strategists has full authority to run a client relationship end to end without her sign-off.
- **A real strategic filter problem:** Erin has half a dozen growth ideas (a productized service tier, a referral partner program, expanding into a new vertical, building an internal AI content tool) and currently picks whichever one whoever pitched it hardest gets her attention that week.

## Why this is a fair second test

Bramblewood is the opposite margin profile from Harbor Fleet: naturally high gross margin, no real cost of goods, the leakage is in subscription bloat and owner-review time, not parts costs or COGS. This tests whether the new industry-aware benchmark in the profit-discovery-audit (Software or other high-margin recurring revenue: 65%/30%) under- or over-credits a business that's already healthy on paper, and whether the other six tools hold up on a services business shaped nothing like a repair shop.
